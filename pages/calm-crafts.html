Intangible Cultural Heritage

Huizhou Fish Lantern
徽州鱼灯

Originating from the Huizhou region of Anhui Province, the bamboo-framed fish lantern is one of China's most treasured folk art traditions. Carried in New Year processions for centuries, the glowing fish symbolises abundance, joy, and the flow of life.

In our workshops, we guide you through the full process — from bending bamboo to painting silk — resulting in a luminous lantern that is entirely your own creation.

Traditional Art Form

Floating Lacquer Art
漆扇 / 流漆

Dating back to the Tang Dynasty, the art of floating lacquer on water — then transferring the swirling patterns onto silk or paper — produces breathtaking results that no machine can replicate. Every piece is a true original.

This technique has recently been rediscovered by a new generation of artisans and is celebrated as one of China's most meditative intangible cultural heritage practices. We think it's also one of the most therapeutic things you can do for an afternoon.
